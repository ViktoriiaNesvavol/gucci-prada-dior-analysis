Fashion Knowledge Project: Wikidata & LLMs

Our Fashion Project: Wikidata & LLMs

Welcome

Our fashion project delves into Gucci, Prada, and Dior, utilizing the rich data available in the Wikidata Knowledge Graph. As a comprehensive and well-structured knowledge base, Wikidata was invaluable for uncovering facts about fashion, especially within the luxury segment. To overcome any data deficiencies, we implemented Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT and Gemini to generate supplementary information. Our team then manually verified and refined these generated insights against the Wikidata ontology to maintain accuracy and coherence.

Why Fashion?

Choosing "fashion" as a topic for our project, particularly through the lens of the Wikidata Knowledge Graph, is driven by its inherent suitability for demonstrating advanced query capabilities and data modeling techniques. The complexities and nuances of the fashion industry provide a rich and challenging domain to apply and illustrate key knowledge engineering principles. Specifically, the "fashion topic" was selected because it allowed us to effectively showcase the power of various SPARQL query components, as outlined in our project methodology. Additionally, we're also building a system that lets us explore this data intuitively.

Methodology

Tool Purpose

GitHub Publication and reporting

Mission:

We're enriching the existing semantic data by:

  • Detecting gaps in the RDF descriptions of drivers.
  • Prompting LLMs to acquire and reformat the necessary missing knowledge.
  • Manually ensuring accuracy through verification and correction of the LLM outputs.
  • Documenting our approach and results clearly for full reproducibility.

Identifying Gaps

Using SPARQL we uncovered several important gaps in Wikidata:

  • Traditional fashion analysis often qualitative
  • Lack of structured, comparative data insights readily available

Outcome

Our project demonstrates a collaborative approach: automated tools working hand-in-hand with human curation to enhance knowledge on open platforms such as Wikidata. All our detailed analysis, case studies, and RDF graphs are available here on our website.
